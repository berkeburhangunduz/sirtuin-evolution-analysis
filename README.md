# Evolutionary Analysis of SIRT1 and SIRT5

## Overview

This project investigates evolutionary selection patterns in SIRT1 and SIRT5 across mammalian species using codon-based evolutionary analyses and structural annotation.

## Methods

- Multiple sequence alignment
- Codon alignment
- Datamonkey FEL analysis
- Datamonkey MEME analysis
- AlphaFold structural mapping
- UniProt functional annotation

## Main Findings

### SIRT1

- FEL positive selection sites: 4
- MEME episodic positive selection sites: 10
- Strong overall purifying selection

### SIRT5

- FEL positive selection sites: 3
- MEME episodic positive selection sites: 6
- Codon 203 identified near Zn²⁺ binding residues (207, 212)

## Biological Interpretation
Both genes are highly conserved.

Positive selection appears to avoid catalytic residues and instead affects regulatory or structural regions.

SIRT5 codon 203 represents a candidate adaptive residue located near a functionally relevant binding environment.


SIRT1 showed more evidence of episodic positive selection than SIRT5
(10 MEME sites vs 6 MEME sites).

In contrast, both proteins retained strong purifying selection across
most codons, indicating conservation of core catalytic functions.

For SIRT5, codon 203 was identified as a positively selected site and
was located near Zn2+ binding residues (207 and 212), suggesting
adaptive evolution may affect local regulatory regions while preserving
the catalytic residue at position 158.
